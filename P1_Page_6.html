Pictured at right is a piece of artwork that I made during my senior year of high school. The prompt was to create a piece that utilized the artistic technique known as "foreshortening," and so I decided to create a composition based on a scene from one of my favorite Spider-Man movies, The Amazing Spider-Man 2.
"Foreshortening" is defined by Encyclopedia Britannica as a "method of rendering a specific object or figure in a picture in depth." This essentially means that the proportions of the subject in a picture are altered to make them appear as though they are existing in a three-dimensional space relative to the perspective of the "viewer" of the artwork. It describes the artistic technique that allow the viewer to experience the piece as if they existed within the moment being depicted and could see from a "real" first-person perspective.
This scene from The Amazing Spider-Man 2 depicts a moment of incredible tragedy and loss for Peter Parker. While fighting his best-friend-gone-mad, Harry Osborn, in an intense aerial battle above a tall clock tower, the life of Peter's girlfriend Gwen Stacy is left literally hanging in the balance as she clings to a webline that is wedged between the failing gears of the clock tower. As Peter struggles to finally subdue Harry, the internals of the clock tower begin to lock up and come to a standstill, with Peter's foot trapped in one of the gears. Peter's weblines cannot hold the tension between them, and so they eventually snap. The metal gears begin to plummet to the ground, freeing Peter's foot, but also sending Gwen into freefall. Peter dives down after her. relying on his web-shooters to send out a webline directly to Gwen Stacy in an attempt to save her life. The webline latches on to Gwen , and Peter comes to a harsh halt in midair as he uses his super-strength to grab the inner scaffolding of the clock tower. Miraculously, none of the falling clock tower gears and shrapnel hit Gwen, but the elasticity of the webbing proves that he made a fatal mistake in catching her. The webbing stops her descent just feet off of the ground, but the webline stretches down further and she suffers a heavy blow to the back of her head.
